# Objection Handling — Carbonfact for Suppliers

## Objection 1: "What about data privacy / confidentiality?"
**Frequency**: #1 most common concern.

**Response**:
> Completely understandable — this is the most common question we get, and it's the reason we built the platform the way we did. Here's the key difference: you never share your actual LCA document with brands. Carbonfact's Science team reviews it internally, and what brands see is only the verified environmental data and system boundary — not the underlying methodology, raw data, or full report. You have a dashboard where you approve or deny every access request, brand by brand. You can also revoke access at any time. And if you need an NDA before sharing anything with us, we're happy to sign one. It's actually more secure than the current PDF-by-email approach.

**Key proof points**: Access-control dashboard, brand-by-brand approval, revocable access, NDA available, no raw LCA shared with brands.

---

## Objection 2: "Why is it free? What's the catch?"

**Response**:
> Fair question. The business model is simple: Carbonfact's paying customers are the 200+ brands who use the platform for carbon accounting and eco-design. When those brands get access to verified supplier data, the platform becomes more valuable for them. So the more high-quality suppliers on the directory, the better the platform works for everyone. That's why it's free for you — you're providing value to the ecosystem, and in return you get visibility, verification, and a structured way to share your data.

**Key proof points**: Brands pay, not suppliers. Network effects — more suppliers = more value for brands = more value for suppliers.

---

## Objection 3: "We already share PDFs directly with our clients."

**Response**:
> That works — until it doesn't. PDFs mean version control issues, repeated NDA processes, email back-and-forth for every new brand request, and no standardization for comparison. With Carbonfact, your data is verified once, structured, and comparable. Brands can access it instantly when they need it — and you avoid repeating the same process for each client. Plus, you become discoverable to brands you're not already working with. It's not replacing your existing relationships — it's making them more efficient and opening new ones.

**Key proof points**: Version control, NDA repetition, no discoverability with PDFs, one-time setup vs recurring effort.

## Objection 5: "This sounds like more work."
**Frequency**: #2 hidden concern — suppliers assume any new platform = more data entry.

**Response**:
> I hear this a lot, and it's the opposite. Right now, every time a brand asks for your environmental data, you're probably sending a PDF, signing an NDA, answering follow-up questions, and repeating it all for the next brand. Carbonfact for Suppliers replaces that cycle. You share your existing LCA documents once — we handle the review, structuring, and normalization. Then your profile becomes the single point where any brand can request access. Instead of 10 separate data-sharing processes, you have one. It's the tool that organizes what you already have and eliminates the repetitive work.

**Key proof points**: One upload → multiple brand relationships. Carbonfact handles structuring. Replaces work, doesn't add it.

## Objection 8: "Our LCA isn't critically reviewed / isn't ready."

**Response**:
> That's fine — a third-party critical review is a nice-to-have in our criteria, but not mandatory. Our Science team will review your LCA for free. If it meets our methodological requirements, we can add you to the directory. Many of our listed suppliers started with an internal or non-reviewed LCA.

**Key proof points**: Free review, critical review not mandatory, quality criteria are reasonable.
