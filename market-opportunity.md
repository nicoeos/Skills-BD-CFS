# Market Opportunity Mapping — Carbonfact for Suppliers

## Regulatory Demand Drivers

| Regulation | Geography | Impact on Suppliers | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| **EU PEF / PEFCR** | EU | Brands need product-level LCA data per category rules. Suppliers with PEF-aligned data get priority. | Ongoing, tightening |
| **French Eco-Score (Textile)** | France | Mandatory environmental labeling for textile products. Brands need granular supplier data. | 2025–2026 rollout |
| **CSRD** | EU | 50,000+ companies must report Scope 3 including supply chain. Suppliers need to provide data. | Phased 2024–2026 |
| **AGEC Law** | France | Requires traceability and environmental information for textile products. | Active |
| **CA Climate Corporate Data Accountability Act** | California, USA | Large companies must report emissions including supply chain. | 2026+ |
| **NY Fashion Act** | New York, USA | Would require fashion companies to map supply chains and disclose environmental impact. | Proposed |

## Mapping Template

When analyzing a specific supplier, answer these questions to define the market angle:

1. **What materials/processes do they produce?**
2. **Which regulated markets are those materials sold into?** (Match to regulation table above)
3. **Which Carbonfact brand clients source similar materials?** (Match to brand segment table)
4. **What regulation deadline creates urgency?** (Specific date or phase)
5. → This becomes the **market angle** in the outreach email — the "[RELEVANT REGULATION]" and "[RELEVANT BRAND NAMES]" placeholders.

### Example mapping:
- Supplier produces recycled polyester yarn
- Sold into EU market (France, Germany, Italy)
- Carbonfact clients sourcing similar: On (outdoor), The North Face (outdoor), Marc O'Polo (mass market)
- Urgency: French Eco-Score rollout 2025–2026, CSRD Scope 3 reporting
- → Email angle: "With the French Eco-Score launching and brands like On actively comparing recycled polyester suppliers on Carbonfact..."
