# LinkedIn Engagement Cadence — Carbonfact for Suppliers

Use LinkedIn as a **parallel channel to email**, not a replacement.

## Timeline

| Timing | Action |
|---|---|
| **Day 0** (before email) | View their profile. Like or comment on a recent post (genuine, relevant comment — not generic). |
| **Day 1** | Send connection request with a short personalized note (no pitch). Example: "Hi [NAME], I work on connecting sustainable suppliers with fashion brands — your work on [MATERIAL] caught my eye. Would love to connect." |
| **Day 3–4** (after Initial email) | If connected: react to their content. Do NOT pitch on LinkedIn yet. Build familiarity. |
| **Day 7–8** (after FU1 email) | If no email reply: send a short LinkedIn DM referencing the email. Keep it to 2–3 sentences. |
| **Day 14+** (after FU2 email) | If still no response: share a relevant piece of content (regulation update, industry trend) as a DM with "thought you'd find this relevant." |
| **Ongoing** | Engage with their content weekly. Stay visible without being pushy. |

## LinkedIn DM Template (Day 7–8)

> Hi [NAME], I sent you a note about Carbonfact for Suppliers — the platform that lets factories like [COMPANY] showcase verified environmental data to 200+ fashion brands. Happy to share a quick example if you're curious. No pressure either way.

## LinkedIn Connection Request Note (Day 1)

> Hi [NAME], your work on [SPECIFIC MATERIAL/INITIATIVE] at [COMPANY] is impressive. I work with suppliers who want their environmental data to reach more brands — would love to connect.

## Content Engagement Guidelines

- **Do**: Comment with substance — reference specific data, ask genuine questions, share relevant perspective
- **Don't**: Drop generic "Great post!" comments, pitch in comments, comment on every single post (1–2 per week max)
- **Best content to engage with**: Posts about sustainability milestones, certifications, new materials, regulation commentary, trade show recaps
